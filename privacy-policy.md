# Eclipse Privacy Policy

Effective date: June 11, 2026

Eclipse is designed to run locally on your device. Eclipse does not operate a user account system, analytics backend, advertising network, or server-side tracking service.

## Data Eclipse Does Not Collect

Eclipse does not collect, sell, rent, or share personal data with the developer.

Eclipse does not collect:

- Your name, email address, phone number, or contacts.
- Precise location.
- Advertising identifiers.
- Analytics events.
- Crash logs sent to a developer-controlled server.
- Reading history, watch history, library items, notes, ratings, downloads, or progress on a developer-controlled server.

## Data Stored On Your Device

Eclipse may store app data locally on your device so the app can function. This may include:

- App settings.
- Library, history, bookmarks, ratings, notes, and progress.
- Reader downloads and cached media or image data.
- Locally saved logs that you can choose to export.
- Authentication tokens for third-party services you choose to connect.

This data is stored by the app on your device and is not sent to the developer.

## Third-Party Services

Eclipse can connect to third-party services only when you choose to use them. These may include media metadata providers, tracker services, external source modules, or reader sources.

When you connect or use a third-party service, your device communicates directly with that service. The service may receive information necessary to complete your request, such as search terms, source requests, authentication tokens, watch progress, reading progress, ratings, or other data you explicitly sync.

Third-party services are governed by their own privacy policies and terms. Eclipse does not control how those services process data.

## Downloads And Offline Content

Downloaded reader or media content is stored locally on your device. Eclipse does not upload downloaded content to the developer.

## Logs

Eclipse may create local diagnostic logs to help troubleshoot app behavior. Logs remain on your device unless you choose to export or share them.

Logs are intended to redact sensitive values such as cookies, tokens, and private headers, but you should review exported logs before sharing them.

## Children's Privacy

Eclipse does not knowingly collect personal data from children.

## Changes To This Policy

This policy may be updated when Eclipse changes how it handles data. The effective date at the top of this page will be updated when changes are made.
